Guidelines~

The use of the fun budget can be roughly split into two categories: spontaneous and planned experiences.

Spontaneous Experiences

A spontaneous experience can be roughly defined as an activity that takes place on the same day it is suggested. Examples might include:

  • Buying ice cream for the office on a hot day
  • Buying an item from a charity shop and gifting it to a colleague

Spontaneous activities are usually quick, cheap and, well, spontaneous. They are intended to occur when someone sees something nice, and wants to share it with other Tribers. Note that it is not required to involve everyone with something spontaneous, although it is always nice to offer. It's all about togetherness!

Reimbursement for spontaneous experiences is easy - all you need to do is talk to Alin: send him a copy of the receipt(s) and the total cost via email.

Planned Experiences

Planned experiences usually take time out of work to do, such as a workshop or escape room. Much of the same rules apply to planned experiences as to spontaneous ones, however there are some differences which will be listed below.

The general guideline for suitable planned experiences are ones that involve at least two colleagues and are fun for them, in addition to at least one of the following:

  • contribute to their well being (e.g. nature walk with a picnic basket, employee appreciation day with pizza)
  • nurture social connections (e.g. birthday karaoke outing)
  • involve team building activities (e.g. climbing, plants in jar workshop)

are given higher priority. The more people involved, the better!

Planning an activity is as easy as suggesting it to Alin, optionally (but preferably!) including a list of people you think would be interested.
